This video starts painting the picture of "what's in it for me?" and "how will my job be more interesting and how can I better serve our customers?"

Imagine what could be accomplished if:
* we knew the range of data that is available
* in which systems the data is stored
* who to contact to access the data
* where to go the if you find data quality issues
* what does the data represent - definitions clear
* use is consistent across all systems
* confident data is accurate and complete
* can connect the different data sets and obtain new insights

Visibility - what data do we have and how used / when captures / stages / where stored?
Authority - who owns the data / manage the data / can change the data?
Consistency - do systems and teams speak the same language / definition of data?
Quality - monitor it / kpis / fix when quality erodes. Change is constant force that wants to erode quality.
Unity - making new connections /integrate/ and insights from data.

What do we actually need to do to improve?

There are many different frameworks and many consulting firms have made lots of money in helping organisations to implement better data governance. The next few videos set out one way of thinking about this. We plan to use some of the ideas in this framework.

 

Key take aways from this video:

Data governance is about ensuring that we have visible, authoratiative and consistent data that we can rely on to get insights to achieve your purpose and goal. Normally this does not mean starting from scratch. We can formalise what we are doing already, fill gaps and get better where required. Key elements are:

1. Strategy and scope. What does governance mean for your organisation (strategy and scope).
2. Roles and responsibilities (who does what? which groups do we use or establish?)
3. Policies (what are the rules and standards? are they coherent)
    > data must be managed as an asset
    > formal accountabilities must be in place
    > data must be compliant with regulatory rules and laws
    > data quality must be defined and managed through life cycle
4. Establish good processes (including escalation)
5. Find tools that support your process (data model / visibility / glosseries / analytics)
6. Communication & training (lots and often)
7. Audit and metrix (track what you are doing!)
